Diagnostic Gaps

Where self-reported effectiveness diverges from operational reality — scoring integrity analysis

Methodology: The Confidence-Adjusted Score recalibrates each studio's raw self-assessment by cross-referencing four operational signals: Delivery Status (Yes = 1.0x, Somewhat = 0.7x, No = 0x), Sub-Capability Coverage (% of sub-capabilities delivered), Constraint Load (number of constraints cited, penalised above 3), and Resource Backing (whether FTE allocation is proportionate to the score). Gaps between raw and adjusted scores surface where self-perception outpaces operational maturity — i.e., where leadership should probe deeper before accepting the self-assessment at face value.

Methodology & Scoring Reference

How the analytical models in this dashboard are constructed — transparency for leadership review

Data Source
All data in this dashboard is sourced from the draftLine 2.0 Capability Diagnostic Survey — a self-assessment instrument completed by 7 studio leads across South Korea, Europe, Canada, Argentina, China, USA, and South Africa. Responses cover effectiveness ratings (1–5 scale), delivery status, FTE allocation, sub-capability coverage, constraint identification, tool usage, tech spend, AI adoption, and governance indicators. All scores are self-reported — no independent verification or external benchmarking has been applied. The analytical models below are designed to cross-reference self-assessments against operational signals to surface credibility gaps and prioritise action.
Model 1: Confidence-Adjusted Score
Purpose: Tests whether a studio's self-reported effectiveness rating (1–5) is supported by operational evidence. Produces an adjusted score that discounts unsupported claims.

Calculation: Starts with the raw effectiveness score and applies a cumulative multiplier based on four signals:
Adjusted Score = Raw Score × Delivery Multiplier × Sub-cap Multiplier × Constraint Multiplier × Resource Multiplier
Delivery StatusYes = ×1.0 | Somewhat/Sometimes = ×0.75 | No = ×0.0
Sub-capability Coverage<50% of sub-caps active = ×0.70 | 50–74% = ×0.85 | ≥75% = ×1.0
Constraint Load≥5 constraints = ×0.80 | 3–4 = ×0.90 | <3 = ×1.0
Resource BackingRating ≥4 but 0 dedicated FTEs = ×0.80 | Otherwise = ×1.0
Used on: Diagnostic Gaps page (heatmap, comparison charts, flagged discrepancies)
Model 2: Operational Maturity Index
Purpose: Measures how operationally established a capability is — independent of self-reported performance. A high maturity score means the foundations (people, process, governance, tools) are in place, regardless of whether the studio rates itself highly.

Calculation: Composite 0–100 index from five weighted components:
Maturity = Delivery (0–25) + Sub-cap Coverage (0–25) + Resource Backing (0–20) + Governance (0–15) + Low Constraints (0–15)
Delivery (25 pts)Yes = 25 | Somewhat = 12 | No = 0
Sub-cap Coverage (25 pts)(Active sub-capabilities / Total possible) × 25
Resource Backing (20 pts)FTE share of total studio headcount × 100, capped at 20. Penalty: −5 if 0 dedicated FTEs and capability is delivered
Governance (15 pts)Crisis process (5 pts) + Martech liaison (5 pts) + ABI importance rating (0–5 pts scaled from 1–3 rating)
Low Constraints (15 pts)0 constraints = 15 | ≤2 = 10 | ≤4 = 5 | >4 = 0
Used on: Maturity vs Performance page (quadrant scatter, studio/capability bars) and as input to Investment Priority Score
Model 3: Investment Priority Score
Purpose: Ranks where incremental investment (headcount, tooling, budget) will generate the highest return across the studio-capability matrix. Higher score = higher priority for investment.

Calculation: Composite 0–100 score from five gap-based signals:
Priority = Perf Gap (0–30) + Maturity Gap (0–25) + Constraint Burden (0–20) + Sub-cap Gap (0–15) + Resource Scarcity (0–10)
Performance Gap (30%)(1 − Confidence-Adjusted Score / 5) × 30. Rewards investing where adjusted performance is lowest.
Maturity Gap (25%)(1 − Maturity Index / 100) × 25. Rewards investing where operational foundations are weakest.
Constraint Burden (20%)(Studio-capability constraints / 7) × 20, capped at 20. Rewards investing where blockers are most concentrated.
Sub-cap Gap (15%)(1 − Active sub-caps / Total) × 15. Rewards investing where capability breadth is narrowest.
Resource Scarcity (10%)0 FTEs = 10 | <3 FTEs = 7 | <6 FTEs = 4 | ≥6 FTEs = 0
Urgency classification: ≥65 = Critical | 50–64 = High | <50 = Medium
Used on: Financial & Resource page (priority bar chart, priority cards)
Model 4: Cost Concentration Risk (HHI)
Purpose: Measures how evenly a studio distributes its FTEs across capabilities. High concentration means the studio is betting on 1–2 capabilities, leaving others structurally unfunded.

Calculation: Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) applied to capability FTE shares:
HHI = Σ (FTEs in capability / Total capability FTEs)² × 10,000
Interpretation: 0 = perfectly even distribution | 10,000 = all FTEs in one capability. Below 2,500 is balanced; 2,500–5,000 is moderate concentration; above 5,000 is high concentration risk.
Used on: Financial & Resource page (concentration risk chart)
Limitations & Caveats
Self-report bias: All effectiveness scores are self-assessed. Studios may over- or under-report based on cultural norms, leadership expectations, or interpretation of the rating scale. The Confidence-Adjusted Score mitigates this partially but cannot eliminate it.

Missing data: China reports 0 FTEs across all capabilities and N/A on several questions. South Korea and Argentina report N/A on tech spend. These gaps affect index calculations — studios with missing data will appear lower on composite indices by default.

No temporal dimension: This is a single point-in-time snapshot. No trend data is available to assess whether capabilities are improving, declining, or stagnating.

Governance signals are studio-level, not capability-level: Crisis process, martech liaison, and ABI importance are assessed once per studio and applied uniformly across all capabilities. In reality, governance maturity may vary by capability.

Weighting assumptions: All model weights (e.g., 30% Performance Gap in the Investment Priority Score) are based on strategic judgment, not empirical calibration. Sensitivity to weight changes has not been tested.
